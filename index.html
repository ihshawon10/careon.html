What is the NDIS?

The NDIS — National Disability Insurance Scheme — is an Australian government program that funds support for people with permanent disability.

If you're eligible, the NDIS gives you a personal plan with funding for the supports you need. You choose how to use that funding and which providers to work with.

Around 600,000 Australians currently receive NDIS funding. You can explore to see how Careon can support your plan.

Am I eligible?

To access the NDIS, you need to be under 65, an Australian citizen or permanent resident, and have a permanent disability that significantly affects your daily life.

Many conditions qualify — autism, cerebral palsy, intellectual disability, hearing loss, vision impairment, and acquired brain injury are common examples.

If you're unsure, give us a call — we're happy to help you work out the next step.

How NDIS plans work

Your NDIS plan sets out your goals and the funding to reach them. There are three ways plans can be managed:

NDIA-Managed: The NDIA pays registered providers like Careon directly from your plan.

Plan-Managed: A plan manager handles invoices and payments. You can use both registered and unregistered providers.

Self-Managed: You control your own funding and choose any provider you like. More flexibility, more responsibility.

Careon accepts all three plan types. — we'll help you make sense of your plan.

in Caroline Springs

March 2026 · 5 min read

Finding the right NDIS provider in Caroline Springs can feel overwhelming — especially if you're new to the NDIS. With so many providers operating across Melbourne's west, knowing what to look for makes a big difference.

The first thing to check is whether the provider is registered. Careon offers and a full range of services across Caroline Springs as a registered provider — which means all three plan management types are accepted.

What to Look For in a Caroline Springs NDIS Provider

Not every NDIS provider is the same. Here are the questions worth asking before you commit:

Do they send the same support worker every time? Consistency matters enormously in disability support. A different face every visit makes it harder to build trust and routine.

Are they available when you need them? Some providers only operate on weekdays. If you need weekend or after-hours support, ask upfront.

Do they accept your plan type? Whether your plan is NDIA-managed, plan-managed, or self-managed, your provider needs to accommodate it. You can learn more in our .
